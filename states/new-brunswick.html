New Brunswick Unclaimed Property

The Financial and Consumer Services Commission (FCNB) of New Brunswick operates the province's Unclaimed Property Program. With over $30 million waiting to be claimed and more than $1 million already returned to owners, there's a significant chance you might have unclaimed funds.

Search New Brunswick Unclaimed Property

Types of Unclaimed Property

Common types of unclaimed property include:

  • Dormant credit union and investment accounts
  • Unclaimed insurance proceeds
  • Uncashed cheques
  • Unclaimed estate assets

How Money Becomes Unclaimed

Property is considered unclaimed when:

  • No account activity for at least 3 years (general property)
  • No activity for 10 years (credit union accounts)
  • You left a job without collecting your last paycheque
  • You received a cheque that you did not cash

How to Search and Claim Your Property

Step 1: Search the Database

Visit FundsFinderNB.ca and enter your name or business name in the search field.

Step 2: Review Results

Check the search results for any matches. The database is updated annually with new unclaimed property.

Step 3: File Your Claim

If you find matching property, submit your claim with the required documentation to prove ownership.

Important Information

The search and claim process is completely free - never pay anyone to help you claim your property

Beware of scammers who may try to charge fees for helping you find unclaimed property
