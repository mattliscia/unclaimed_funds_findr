What Is Unclaimed Property?

Unclaimed property refers to financial assets that have been abandoned or forgotten. Common examples in Alaska include:

  • Dormant savings or checking accounts
  • Uncashed payroll or dividend checks
  • Safe deposit box contents
  • Insurance payouts
  • Stock certificates or refunds

These assets are turned over to the state for safekeeping until they are claimed by the rightful owner or their heirs.

How to Search for and Claim Your Funds

Step 1: Conduct a Search

Start by visiting Alaska's official unclaimed property website

Enter your name or business name in the search tool to see if there are any assets linked to you.

Step 2: File Your Claim

If you find property belonging to you, follow the on-screen prompts to file a claim. You'll need to:

  • Provide personal information, including your mailing address
  • Select your relationship to the property (e.g., owner or heir)

Step 3: Submit Required Documentation

Most claims require:

  • A completed and signed claim form
  • A copy of photo identification (e.g., driver's license)
  • A document showing your social security number (e.g., SSN card, W-2 form)

To determine your exact documentation needs, refer to the email you'll receive after filing your claim.

For quickest processing, upload your documents via Alaska's secure portal using the link provided in the email. If you have questions, contact the Treasury Division for assistance.

Step 4: Check Your Claim Status

Once your claim is submitted, track its progress using the Claim Status tool on the website.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Is there a fee to search for or claim unclaimed property in Alaska?

No, Alaska offers this service free of charge. Avoid any third-party services that charge fees for assistance.

2. How long does it take to process a claim?

Processing times vary depending on the complexity of the claim and the documentation submitted. Ensure all required documents are complete to avoid delays.

3. Can non-residents claim funds from Alaska?

Yes, you can claim property from Alaska even if you no longer reside there, as long as you are the rightful owner or heir.
