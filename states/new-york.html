New York Unclaimed Property

Under the guidance of Comptroller Thomas P. DiNapoli, New York State's Unclaimed Property program is dedicated to reuniting individuals and businesses with their lost funds. With over $19 billion in unclaimed money currently being held, the state returns approximately $1.5 million every day to rightful owners.

Types of Unclaimed Property

Unclaimed property can come from various sources, including:

  • Bank accounts
  • Uncashed checks
  • Utility deposits
  • Insurance benefits
  • Stocks and dividends

How to Search and Claim Your Property

Step 1: Search the Database

Visit the New York State Comptroller's website to search by name or business name.

Step 2: File Your Claim

Once you've located unclaimed property, filing a claim is easy. The state will work with you to verify your ownership.

Step 3: Receive Your Funds

After verification, the state will return your funds without charge.
