Minnesota Unclaimed Property

The Minnesota Department of Commerce Unclaimed Property Division manages the state's unclaimed property program. As of now, they have returned more than $814 million in unclaimed property to Minnesota residents.

Search Minnesota Unclaimed Property

What Is Unclaimed Property?

Unclaimed property in Minnesota may include:

  • Bank accounts
  • Checks
  • Stocks and dividends
  • Uncashed insurance policies
  • Safe deposit box contents
  • Escrow funds

How to Claim Your Property

Step 1: Search the Database

Visit the Minnesota Unclaimed Property website to search for unclaimed property by entering your name, business name, or the name of a deceased relative.

Step 2: Submit a Claim

If you find unclaimed property, follow the steps to submit a claim. You will need to provide identifying information and documents proving your ownership.

Step 3: Provide Documentation

  • Photo ID (such as a driver's license)
  • Proof of address
  • Proof of ownership (e.g., a utility bill, tax return, or birth certificate)

Step 4: Receive Your Property

Once your claim is reviewed and processed, the unclaimed property will be returned to you.

Check Back Often!

New unclaimed property is reported regularly, so it's a good idea to check periodically to see if any property belonging to you has been reported.
