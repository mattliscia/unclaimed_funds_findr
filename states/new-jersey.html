New Jersey Unclaimed Property

The New Jersey Unclaimed Property Administration (UPA), led by Administrator Steven Harris, has returned over $2.7 billion to rightful owners. The Administration focuses on reuniting individuals with a wide range of unclaimed assets.

Types of Unclaimed Property

Unclaimed property is typically held for a minimum of three years before being transferred to the State Treasury. Common types include:

  • Bank accounts
  • Stocks and dividends
  • Insurance benefits
  • Wages
  • Security deposits

How to Search and Claim Your Property

Step 1: Search the Database

Visit the New Jersey Unclaimed Property Administration website and enter your name or business name to begin your search.

Step 2: File Your Claim

If you find property belonging to you, follow the online instructions to submit your claim.

Step 3: Provide Documentation

Submit the required documentation to verify your identity and ownership of the property.
