Unclaimed Property in Kentucky

Kentucky's Unclaimed Property Fund is managed by the Kentucky State Treasurer and contains nearly $800 million in unclaimed assets belonging to individuals and local government agencies.

Search Kentucky Unclaimed Property

What Is Unclaimed Property?

Unclaimed property typically includes:

  • Payroll checks
  • Safe deposit box contents
  • Old life insurance policies
  • Stocks & bonds
  • Vendor checks

After three years of inactivity, financial institutions and businesses are required to transfer unclaimed assets to the Kentucky State Treasury.

How to Search for and Claim Your Property

Step 1: Search for Property

Use the Kentucky State Treasury's Unclaimed Property Database to search by name. You can also check MissingMoney.com, a national unclaimed property database.

Step 2: Submit a Claim

If you find property in your name, follow the steps to claim it and provide proof of identity and ownership.

Step 3: Track Your Claim

If you have already filed a claim, you can track its progress online through the Kentucky unclaimed property portal.

Due Diligence Notices

  • Throughout the year, businesses send notices to owners of unclaimed property.
  • If you receive such a notice, contact the company directly to claim your funds.
  • If you do not claim the funds within 60 days, they are turned over to the Kentucky State Treasury.
