Michigan Unclaimed Property

The Michigan Department of Treasury manages the state's Unclaimed Property Program. Over the past 5 years, the state has returned more than $600 million in unclaimed assets to rightful owners.

Search Michigan Unclaimed Property

What Is Considered Unclaimed Property?

Unclaimed property in Michigan can include:

  • Bank accounts
  • Checks
  • Stocks and dividends
  • Insurance policies
  • Safe deposit box contents
  • Escrow funds
  • Utility deposits

How to Claim Your Property

Step 1: Search the Database

Visit the Michigan Unclaimed Property website to search for unclaimed funds by entering your name, business name, or the name of a deceased relative.

Step 2: Submit a Claim

After identifying property, you'll need to complete a claim form and provide required documentation to prove your identity and ownership, which may include a driver's license, utility bills, or other official records.

Step 3: Receive Your Property

Once your claim is processed and verified, Michigan will return the unclaimed property to you.

Keep Checking!

New properties are reported each year, so be sure to check regularly for any unclaimed assets that may belong to you.
