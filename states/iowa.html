The Great Iowa Treasure Hunt

The Great Iowa Treasure Hunt is Iowa's unclaimed property program, managed by the State Treasurer's Office. To date, the program has successfully returned over $390 million to rightful owners.

Search Iowa Unclaimed Property

What Is Unclaimed Property?

Unclaimed property includes financial assets such as:

  • Forgotten bank accounts
  • Uncashed checks
  • Insurance benefits
  • Utility security deposits
  • Safe deposit box contents

These assets are turned over to the State Treasurer's Office when businesses lose contact with the owner after a specific period.

How to Search for and Claim Your Property

Step 1: Search for Property

Visit the official Great Iowa Treasure Hunt website. Enter your last name or business name to begin your search. Including a first name or city can help refine the results.

Step 2: Submit a Claim

If you identify property belonging to you, follow the on-screen instructions to initiate a claim. You will need to register for an account to proceed.

Step 3: Provide Required Documentation

  • Clear copies of current state or federal-issued photo identification
  • Documentation proving ownership of the property (bank statements, utility bills, etc.)

Step 4: Track Your Claim

Monitor your claim status online using your claim ID. If you've lost this number, contact the Unclaimed Property Division at FoundIt@tos.iowa.gov or (515) 281-5367.

Preventing Future Unclaimed Property

  • Regularly update your contact information with financial institutions
  • Keep accurate records of all your accounts and financial assets
  • Cash all checks promptly
  • Inform a trusted person about the location of your financial records and assets
