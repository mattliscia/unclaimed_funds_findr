Unclaimed Property in Illinois

The Illinois State Treasurer's Office manages the state's Unclaimed Property Program, reconnecting residents and businesses with lost or forgotten assets. Since 2015, the Treasurer's Office has successfully returned over $2 billion in cash and assets.

Illinois' program, formerly known as ICash, allows individuals, businesses, and estates to search and claim property online. The process is straightforward, and there is no cost to file a claim.

What Is Unclaimed Property?

Unclaimed property includes a variety of financial assets, such as:

  • Dormant bank accounts
  • Uncashed paychecks
  • Insurance proceeds
  • Stocks, bonds, and dividends
  • Safe deposit box contents
  • Refunds and rebates

These assets are turned over to the state after a period of inactivity when the rightful owner cannot be located.

How to Search for and Claim Your Property

Step 1: Search for Property

Visit Illinois' unclaimed property database and enter your name or business name.

Step 2: Select Property and Begin Your Claim

Review the search results and choose the property(ies) you wish to claim. Follow the on-screen instructions to start the claim process.

Step 3: Submit Required Documentation

Each claim requires verification documents, which may include:

  • A government-issued photo ID (e.g., driver's license or passport)
  • Proof of ownership, such as a bank statement, pay stub, or official letter
  • Additional documents for business or estate claims

Step 4: Track Your Claim

Illinois allows claimants to monitor the status of their claim online. Most claims are processed within a few weeks, though complex cases may take longer.
