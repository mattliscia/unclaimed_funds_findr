Unclaimed Property in Kansas

Kansas' unclaimed property program is managed by the State Treasurer's Office, which has returned over $429 million to rightful owners.

Search Kansas Unclaimed Property

What Is Unclaimed Property?

Unclaimed property refers to financial assets turned over to the state when businesses lose contact with the rightful owner. Common types include:

  • Payroll checks
  • Refunds & overpayments
  • Money orders
  • Bank accounts
  • Stocks & bonds
  • Safe deposit box contents
  • Insurance proceeds

How to Search for and Claim Your Property

Step 1: Search for Property

Visit the Kansas State Treasurer's Unclaimed Property website. Enter your last name or business name to begin your search. You can refine results by adding your first name or city.

Step 2: Submit a Claim

If you find property in your name, follow the instructions to initiate a claim. You'll need to provide proof of identity and ownership.

Step 3: Provide Required Documentation

  • State or federal-issued photo ID (Driver's License, Passport, etc.)
  • Proof of ownership (old bank statements, utility bills, insurance policies, etc.)

Step 4: Track Your Claim

After submitting your claim, you can monitor its status online through the Kansas unclaimed property portal.

Important Notes

  • The Kansas State Treasurer never charges a fee to claim your unclaimed property.
  • New unclaimed property is reported annually, so check back regularly.
