Wisconsin Unclaimed Property

The Wisconsin Department of Revenue is responsible for managing unclaimed property in the state. Wisconsin works to reunite residents with forgotten or unclaimed funds that businesses and institutions are required to report after a certain period of inactivity.

Search Wisconsin Unclaimed Property

Types of Unclaimed Property

Unclaimed property can include a variety of assets, such as:

  • Uncashed checks
  • Bank accounts
  • Refunds
  • Insurance proceeds
  • Security deposits
  • Stocks and dividends

How It Works

Property is considered "unclaimed" when it has been left untouched for a period of time, and businesses or institutions are unable to locate the rightful owner. These assets are transferred to the state for safekeeping.

Important: The search and claim process is completely free!

How to Search and Claim Your Property

Step 1: Search the Database

Visit the Wisconsin Unclaimed Property website and use the search tool to enter your name or business name.

Step 2: File Your Claim

Once you find your unclaimed property, you can file a claim through the website. The process is simple, free, and can be done online.

Step 3: Verify Your Identity

Provide the required documentation to prove your identity and claim to the property.
