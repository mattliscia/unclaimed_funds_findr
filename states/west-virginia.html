West Virginia Unclaimed Property

The West Virginia State Treasurer's Office, led by Larry Pack, works to reunite the rightful owners with their lost or forgotten property. The state holds unclaimed property in various forms, including bank accounts, uncashed checks, insurance policies, and more.

Search West Virginia Unclaimed Property

Types of Unclaimed Property

Unclaimed property includes anything of value that has been left behind or forgotten, such as:

  • Uncashed checks
  • Security deposits
  • Forgotten bank accounts
  • Unclaimed insurance policy proceeds
  • Unclaimed refunds

How It Works

If there is no activity on these assets for a certain period of time, they are turned over to the state and held until the rightful owner comes forward to claim them.

Important: The process is completely free and secure!

How to Search and Claim Your Property

Step 1: Search the Database

Visit the West Virginia Unclaimed Property Search page and enter your name, business name, or other relevant details.

Step 2: File Your Claim

If you find a match, you can file a claim to retrieve your unclaimed property. The process is free and straightforward.

Step 3: Provide Documentation

Submit the required documentation to verify your identity and ownership of the property.
