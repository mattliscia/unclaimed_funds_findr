Unclaimed Property in Georgia

The Georgia Department of Revenue oversees the state's unclaimed property program, connecting individuals and businesses with lost or forgotten financial assets. The program is entirely free to use, allowing claimants to search, file claims, and track the status of their unclaimed property.

Search Georgia Unclaimed Property

What Is Unclaimed Property?

Unclaimed property consists of financial assets that have been left inactive or abandoned, such as:

  • Wages or payroll checks
  • Savings accounts
  • Customer refunds
  • Insurance payments
  • Stocks, bonds, or dividends
  • Safe deposit box contents

How to Search for and Claim Your Property

Step 1: Search for Property

Visit Georgia's unclaimed property database.

You can search using:

  • Your name
  • Estate's name or FEIN
  • Business name and FEIN

Step 2: Submit a Claim

To file a claim:

  • Register for a free account on Georgia's portal
  • Submit claims for yourself, business, or as an heir/executor
  • Complete required claim forms for estates or businesses

Step 3: Track Your Claim

Most claims are processed within 30 days, though some may take up to 90 days. Monitor your claim status through Georgia's online portal.

Step 4: Receive Your Claim

Approved claims are paid by paper check and mailed to your registered address. Electronic payments are not available.
