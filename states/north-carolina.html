North Carolina Unclaimed Property

The North Carolina State Treasurer's office is dedicated to reuniting residents with their unclaimed property. Through a secure and free service, the state works to ensure that unclaimed funds, from forgotten bank accounts to uncashed checks, are returned to their rightful owners.

Search North Carolina Unclaimed Property

Types of Unclaimed Property

Property becomes unclaimed when there has been no contact or transaction with the owner for an extended period. Common types include:

  • Bank accounts
  • Uncashed checks
  • Utility deposits
  • Wages
  • Insurance benefits

Search Tips

  • Enter your last name or business name in the search field
  • Narrow results by including your first name, city, or zip code
  • Try alternative spellings, nicknames, or maiden names if initial search yields no results

Filing Your Claim

Step 1: Select Your Property

After finding your unclaimed property, select the items you wish to claim.

Step 2: File Your Claim

Click "Continue To File Claim" to proceed with the filing process.

Step 3: Verify Information

You'll be asked to verify your relationship to the property and provide claimant information.
