Vermont Unclaimed Property

The Vermont State Treasurer's Office works to reunite Vermont residents with over $119 million in unclaimed property. The state holds a vast database containing more than 900,000 unclaimed properties, ranging from forgotten bank accounts to the contents of safe deposit boxes.

Search Vermont Unclaimed Property

Types of Unclaimed Property

Unclaimed property refers to any financial asset owed to an individual, business, or nonprofit that has been held by a company for a certain amount of time without any contact from the owner. Common types include:

  • Forgotten bank accounts
  • Uncashed paychecks
  • Unrefunded security deposits
  • Stocks returned by the US Postal Service
  • Mutual funds and IRAs
  • Contents of safe deposit boxes

Claim Process

Online Claims: For properties under $200, an online claim can be filed and processed quickly, usually within two business days.

Mail Claims: For claims over $200, print the claim form and mail it with the necessary documentation.
