New Mexico Unclaimed Property

The Unclaimed Property Unit of New Mexico is responsible for safeguarding abandoned or unclaimed funds, which are surrendered to the state by financial institutions, insurance companies, utilities, and other businesses. These funds help support the state's general fund while waiting to be claimed by rightful owners.

Search New Mexico Unclaimed Property

Types of Unclaimed Property

Common types of unclaimed property in New Mexico include:

  • Bank accounts
  • Insurance benefits
  • Utility deposits
  • Investment accounts
  • Unpaid wages

Tips for Searching

  • Search for individuals, family members, or businesses using last names and partial first names if needed
  • Include city and/or state information to narrow your search, especially with common names
  • Claims for deceased family members can only be filed by the rightful heir

Additional Search Resources

In addition to searching the New Mexico database, you can:

  • Use MissingMoney.com to search across multiple states
  • Check the U.S. Department of the Treasury's Financial Management Service website for federal unclaimed assets
