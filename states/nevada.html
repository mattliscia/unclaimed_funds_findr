Nevada Unclaimed Property

The Nevada State Treasurer's Office is responsible for managing unclaimed property, ensuring that funds and items, once thought abandoned, are safely returned to their rightful owners. To date, Nevada has returned more than $710 million in unclaimed property to its rightful owners.

Search Nevada Unclaimed Property

What Is Unclaimed Property?

Unclaimed property includes various types of forgotten assets such as:

  • Forgotten bank accounts
  • Uncashed checks
  • Abandoned safe deposit box contents

Note: Due to recent updates to the state's financial system, there may be some delays in processing claims.

How It Works

Business Reporting

Nevada businesses and institutions are required by law to transfer unclaimed assets to the State Treasurer after a specified period of inactivity.

State's Role

The office acts solely as a custodian of these items and funds, never taking ownership. The property remains available for claim by the rightful owner indefinitely.

How to Claim Your Property

Step 1: Search the Database

Step 2: File Your Claim
