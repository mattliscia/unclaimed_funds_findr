What Is Unclaimed Property?

Unclaimed property includes assets that have been abandoned or forgotten due to inactivity. Examples include:

  • Forgotten bank accounts
  • Uncashed checks, such as payroll or dividends
  • Refunds, rebates, or utility deposits
  • Insurance policy proceeds
  • Safe deposit box contents

The Arkansas State Auditor's office safeguards these funds until they are claimed by their rightful owners or heirs.

How to Search for and Claim Your Funds

Step 1: Conduct a Search

Begin your search by visiting Arkansas's unclaimed property website.

Enter your name, a business name, or other relevant details to check for unclaimed property.

Step 2: File Your Claim

Once you've located property that belongs to you:

  • Select the property you wish to claim
  • Review the claim details and click File Claim to proceed

Step 3: Provide Claimant Information

Complete your personal details, including:

  • Your name and contact information
  • Current mailing address (this is where your funds will be sent)
  • Your relationship to the property (e.g., owner, heir, or representative)

Step 4: Submit Required Documentation

Most claims require:

  • A signed claim form
  • A copy of your photo ID (e.g., driver's license or passport)
  • Proof of social security number (e.g., SSN card or tax document)

Upload these documents via Arkansas's secure portal for faster processing.

Step 5: Track Your Claim Status

You can check your claim's progress at any time through Arkansas's online portal.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Is there a cost to claim unclaimed property in Arkansas?

No, the service is free of charge. Avoid third-party services that may charge fees for this free government-provided service.

2. Can businesses claim unclaimed property?

Yes, businesses can also claim property listed under their name by providing the required documentation.

3. How long does it take to process a claim?

Claims are typically processed within a few weeks, depending on the complexity of the claim and the documentation provided.
