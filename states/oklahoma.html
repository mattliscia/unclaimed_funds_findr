Oklahoma Unclaimed Property

The Oklahoma State Treasurer's Unclaimed Property Program is dedicated to returning lost or forgotten property to its rightful owners. From cash to jewelry, stock certificates, and even coin collections, the state safeguards unclaimed property to ensure it is returned to those who rightfully own it.

Legal Framework

Oklahoma operates under the Uniform Unclaimed Property Act (Title 60, section 651 et seq.). Reports on unclaimed property are published twice a year in local newspapers and are available for public access through the Oklahoma Supreme Court Network or Legislative Service Bureau.

Types of Unclaimed Property

Property becomes unclaimed when there has been:

  • Lack of activity from the rightful owner
  • Failure to cash a check
  • Return of a check as undeliverable
  • No communication from the owner

How to Search and Claim Your Property

Step 1: Online Search

Visit the Oklahoma State Treasurer's Unclaimed Property Program website to search for your name or business.

Step 2: Submit Your Claim

Begin the claims process directly through the site to save time on paperwork. Alternatively, you can download the Unclaimed Property Inquiry Form if you prefer a formal inquiry.

Step 3: Provide Documentation

Submit the required documentation to verify your identity and claim to the property.
